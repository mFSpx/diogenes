# Glow Hunter Seedlist

Purpose: seed the anomaly library for people, organizations, and methods that appear to outperform institutional workflows or expose institutional laziness.

Glow is not celebrity. Glow is **method superiority with receipts**.

## Scoring dimensions

1. Anomalous output quality
2. Unusual method
3. Repeatability
4. Receipts/evidence trail
5. Productive backlash / institutional discomfort
6. Teachability
7. Ethical survivability
8. Usefulness to a one-person investigative force

## Initial seed profiles to research deeply

### Bellingcat / Eliot Higgins

- Domain: OSINT / investigative journalism
- Glow pattern: transparent methods, reproducible open-source evidence, public archiving.
- Indy move: always show work; preserve source; let evidence trails be inspectable.

### I. F. Stone

- Domain: document-driven journalism
- Glow pattern: read public records obsessively while access journalists chased proximity.
- Indy move: documents beat vibes; public records can embarrass insiders.

### Ida Tarbell

- Domain: investigative history / monopoly reporting
- Glow pattern: combine documents, interviews, timelines, and system-level structure.
- Indy move: timeline the machine, not just the villain.

### Nardwuar

- Domain: interview research / cultural journalism
- Glow pattern: artifact-specific preparation so deep it destabilizes canned answers.
- Indy move: prepare with precise artifacts, not generic questions.

### Hunter S. Thompson

- Domain: gonzo narrative
- Glow pattern: immersion and voice as truth instruments.
- Indy move: use voice for product energy, but keep evidence/inference boundaries strict.

### John Boyd / OODA

- Domain: strategy / decision loops
- Glow pattern: orientation and tempo as advantage.
- Indy move: ask what loop the operator is inside and what decision changes next.

### Barbara Minto / issue-tree consulting style

- Domain: structured thinking
- Glow pattern: answer-first structure, issue trees, MECE decomposition.
- Indy move: turn chaos into answer-first memos.

### Excellent fraud examiners

- Domain: fraud investigation
- Glow pattern: boring, precise, non-accusatory reports that decision-makers can use.
- Indy move: findings, not verdicts; evidence, not theater.
